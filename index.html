Managing Your Depression

Our mission here is to help people who are suffering from depression to be able to point out one's symptoms and provide support to those who need it.

5 Causes & Why It Happens

The causes of depression are many, but if you can understand those causes and why they happen or where they originate from, you will have a better chance of overcoming it. Although depression is sometimes a day to day struggle, here are some things that make the struggle even harder: Medications can most definitely help a person, but there are certain ones out there that can worsen one's depression. (This is when you should seek a medical professional). Medication does not cure your depression, but it will help assist you if you are taking it consistantly (as perscribed).

Symptoms-How to Tell If You Are Suffering From Depression

There are multiple symptoms that can occur if someone is suffering from depression. Loss of interest in activites or avoiding friends and family. If you find yourself excessively sleeping, feeling hopeless, no energy or motivation to do every day things; these are all signs of depression. If you notice you have a loss of appetite, start binge eatting, or more importantly, if you are having suicial/homicidal thoughts or ideations, please please seek emergency professional help. Your life is worth something, don't forget that.

Statistics & Facts

Women are more likely to have depression than men. An estimated 3.8% of the population experience depression, including 5% of adults (4% among men and 6% among women), and 5.7% of adults older than 60 years. Approximately 280 million people in the world have depression. Here are facts you should know about depression: 1.There isn't always a quantifiable reason for one's depression. 2.Many factors may cause depression. 3.Children get depression too. 4.It is a real illness. 5.It's different than sadness. 6.The depression umbrella comes in many aspects. 7.Depression can change how we think. 8.Depression can lead to other health issues. 9.Woman are more susceptible. 10.DEPRESSION CAN BE TREATED!
